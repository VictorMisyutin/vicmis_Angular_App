What are Neural Networks?

A neural network is a machine learning model inspired by the structure and function of the human brain. Just like how the human brain has neurons connected together, a neural network has layers of interconnected nodes. Each node is called a neuron. When a neuron is activated, it sends a signal to the neurons connected to it. The way the neurons are connected and how the signal is sent is determined by the weights and biases of each neuron.

The way the neurons are organized is determined by the architecture of the neural network. The simplest architecture is a feedforward network, where the neurons are organized in layers and the neurons in each layer are connected to the neurons in the next layer. There are also more complex architectures like convolutional and recurrent neural networks.

The process of training a neural network is called backpropagation. First, the neural network is given some data to train on. Then, the neural network makes a prediction based on the data. The prediction is then compared to the actual output and the difference is calculated. The difference is then used to adjust the weights and biases of each neuron. This process is repeated until the difference between the prediction and the actual output is small enough.

Neural networks have many applications in computer science, including image recognition, natural language processing, and game playing.
